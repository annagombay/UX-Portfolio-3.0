THE PROBLEM

ESR's website lacked user-centricity

Over the course of several years, ESR and their user base had evolved but their promotional website hadn't.

The existing design did not capitalize on ESR's most valuable offerings and risked being out of sync with their contemporary users' interests.

DESIGNING A SOLUTION

Gathering user insights

With a limited research budget, I needed a creative way to learn about the current users' needs and pain points.

As such, I scoured through hundreds of recent user emails and synthesized their discourse into broad themes. I validated my research through consultations with the company's CEO and my senior colleagues.

Safety, community, and competition were the most salient themes I extracted, and they became the foundation of my design approach as I aimed to enhance the user-centricity of the website.

Emphasizing safety

To address safety, I promoted the app's selling feature: CRASHLIGHT.

CRASHLIGHT is a safety feature developed by ESR that automatically detects motorcycle crashes and notifies up to three contacts with the rider's exact location via SMS (text) message, automated phone call, and email.

I devoted a full web-page using a high contrast background colour to bring attention to this feature. I also incorporated two hyperlinks and included a design element advertising CRASHLIGHT's 30-day free trial to encourage users to learn more about the offering.

Fostering community

To foster a sense of community, I emphasized the crowdsourced nature and global reach of the app.

I included real posts from the app to give users a sense of the conversations happening... and superimposed over a world map displaying datapoints for the company's users... I emphasized the ability to meet like minded people. designed features that encouraged social interaction and collaboration among users. This involved implementing user profiles, a messaging system, and discussion forums where users could connect, share experiences, and seek support from like-minded individuals. By facilitating these connections, we created a vibrant and supportive community within the website, enhance the view of user engagement and satisfaction.

Encouraging competition

Understanding the competitive aspect desired by the user base, I incorporated elements that promoted healthy competition and achievement. This included ... a frame about leaderboards and monthly prizes... leaderboards, challenges, and achievement badges that allowed users to showcase their progress and compare themselves with others. By tapping into users' competitive drive, we created a dynamic and motivating environment that encouraged them to push their limits and strive for excellence.
