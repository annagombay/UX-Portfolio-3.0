THE PROBLEM

ESR's website lacked user-centricity

Over the course of several years, ESR and their user base had evolved but their promotional website hadn't.

The existing design did not capitalize on ESR's most valuable offerings and risked being out of sync with their contemporary users' interests.

DESIGNING A SOLUTION

Gathering user insights

With a limited research budget, I needed a creative way to learn about users' needs and pain points.

As such, I scoured through hundreds of recent user emails and synthesized their discourse into broad themes. To validate my findings, I sought the guidance of our company's CEO as well as my senior colleagues.

Safety, community, and competition were the most salient themes I extracted, and they became the foundation of my design approach as I aimed to enhance the user-centricity of the website.

Emphasizing user safety

To underscore user safety, I promoted the app's selling feature: CRASHLIGHT©.

CRASHLIGHT© is a safety feature developed by ESR that automatically detects motorcycle crashes and notifies pre-set contacts of the rider's exact location.

I devoted a full web-page with a high contrast background to bring attention to this feature. I also incorporated two hyperlinks and a design element advertising CRASHLIGHT©'s 30-day free trial to encourage users to pursue the offering.

Cultivating a community

To foster a sense of community, I highlighted the crowdsourced nature and global reach of the app.

I included real community posts from app users and superimposed this content over a map displaying datapoints that represent user locations worldwide.

Encouraging playful competition

To inspire healthy competition, I advertised the app's games and challenges.

I showcased a leaderboard distinguishing riders by tiers of acheivement to signal the platform's recognition of varying levels of skill and dedication.

I also drew attention to the challenge prizes, demonstrating users' capacity to reap tangible rewards for coming out on top.
